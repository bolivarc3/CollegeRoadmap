Career and College Prep Roadmap – Junior Year

Phase 1

Self-Discovery

Begin your journey by understanding yourself better.

  • Study for the ACT/SAT
  • Take practice tests or tutoring
  • College and career research
  • Visit campuses and explore programs
  • Internships, volunteering, and job shadowing
  • Apply for scholarships early
  • Attend college fairs & network with reps

Month-by-Month Breakdown

  • August–September: Identify your interests and values, explore general career areas
  • October–November: Explore branches of interest and talk to professionals
  • December–January: Evaluate what excites you, reflect and begin narrowing
  • February–March: Finalize 2–3 majors and careers to focus on
  • April–May: Research colleges with strong programs and finalize a direction

Step 1: Self-Assessment

Use these tools to discover your strengths and interests:

  • O*NET Interest Profiler – My Next Move: Identify career interests based on activities you enjoy
  • Holland Code (RIASEC) Test: Categorizes your personality type for career matching
  • 16Personalities: Myers-Briggs based personality assessment with career suggestions
  • Enneagram: Identifies core motivations and personality patterns
  • Positivity Project: Focuses on character strengths
  • Work Values Test: Identifies what matters most to you in a workplace

Step 2: Career Exploration

  • Use O*NET, CareerOneStop, and the Occupational Outlook Handbook
  • Conduct informational interviews via LinkedIn, friends, or family
  • Make a list of specific jobs that interest you
  • Explore broad career clusters (STEM, business, healthcare, etc.)
  • Investigate specific jobs: duties, salary, job outlook

Step 3: Narrowing Down

Evaluate education paths, job market demand, and align with your values.

  • List potential majors and careers
  • Evaluate education needs, cost, job demand
  • Start a passion project based on what you've learned
  • Reflect frequently as your plans take shape
