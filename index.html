Junior Year Roadmap

  • Study for the ACT/SAT, take practice tests, and consider tutoring if needed.
  • Take the ACT/SAT early to allow time for retakes if necessary.
  • Research colleges, visit campuses, and compare programs.
  • Explore career interests through internships, volunteer work, and job shadowing.
  • Attend college fairs and networking events to engage with admissions representatives.
  • Apply for scholarships early and frequently.

Senior Year Roadmap

  • Finalize a list of colleges and prioritize choices based on financial aid, programs, and location.
  • Write and refine college essays, tailoring them to each school.
  • Request letters of recommendation from teachers, counselors, or employers.
  • Submit applications before deadlines, ensuring accuracy and completeness.
  • Complete FAFSA and research additional financial aid opportunities.
  • Visit campuses for admitted students' events and make a final decision.
  • Prepare for college life by organizing housing, meal plans, and essential supplies.

Application Process

Colleges have different application processes, including Common App, Coalition App, and school-specific applications. Understand requirements, deadlines, and fees associated with each.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

Types of aid include merit-based, need-based, and private scholarships. Apply to local and national scholarships, and be mindful of deadlines.

Choosing the Right College

Consider factors such as class sizes, professor-to-student ratio, academic programs, extracurricular activities, and career placement opportunities.

Extracurricular Activities

Colleges value well-rounded students. Engage in leadership roles, clubs, sports, music, or community service to stand out in applications.

College Essays & Interviews

Essays should showcase personal growth, experiences, and aspirations. Prepare for interviews by practicing common questions and articulating your goals confidently.

Housing & Meal Plans

Research on-campus and off-campus housing options. Understand dorm life policies, roommate selection, and meal plan flexibility.

Time Management & Study Tips

Use planners, set deadlines, and maintain a balanced study schedule. Form study groups and attend tutoring sessions for additional support.

Mental Health & Well-Being

College can be stressful, so access campus counseling services, maintain a support network, and practice self-care techniques.

Internships & Job Preparation

Gain work experience through internships, co-op programs, or part-time jobs. Build a strong resume and practice interview skills.

Graduation & Beyond

Plan ahead for post-graduation opportunities, including further education, job search strategies, and networking events.
