Writing Your Essay

  • Opening Hook: Start with a compelling anecdote or question
  • Central Theme: Keep the essay focused and intentional
  • Key Experiences: Highlight growth moments
  • Unique Perspective: Showcase your voice and journey
  • Closing Reflection: Leave a strong, thoughtful impression

The Formula for Success

Personal Object + Classical Theme + Secret Theme = Great Essay

Example: Bonsai Tree + Perseverance + Inner Tranquility

  • Reflect on what symbols or stories define you
  • Choose unique objects that represent your journey
  • Connect to universal themes that admissions officers recognize
  • Add a personal "secret theme" that makes your story unique

Hooks & Conclusions

  • Hooks: Use conflict, mystery, or unique experiences
  • Conclusion: Address "So what?" and how you'll contribute to campus
  • Contribution Examples: Tech skills, curiosity, leadership, cultural perspective

Tips from ElevatEd

  • Avoid clichés: Sports injuries, tragedies, regurgitated achievements
  • Choose unique themes: Humbling experiences, personal failures, moments of growth
  • Multiple drafts: Expect 7-8 drafts for the final version
  • Be authentic: Show, don't tell your story
  • Start early: Give yourself time for feedback and revision
  • Seek feedback: From mentors and peers who know you well
